# MedCamp Hardware Recommendations

All software runs in a web browser — no app installs required on any device. Hardware selections below are optimized for reliability at a half-day, 300–500 patient event.

---

## Label Printer

The label printer is the most critical piece of hardware. It needs to handle four label sizes from one roll without a roll change mid-camp.

**Recommended: Brother QL-1110NWB** (~$180)
- WiFi + USB connected — works at the registration desk and is accessible from any tablet on the network
- Prints up to 4" wide on continuous roll — handles all label types below
- Fast: ~70 labels/min
- No ink cartridges — thermal printing

**Label roll to use: Brother DK-22205** (2.4" continuous roll, white)
- Cut to size per label type — the system controls the cut length
- One roll handles all four label types without swapping

| Label | Dimensions | Qty per patient |
|---|---|---|
| Patient badge | 2.4" × 4" | 1 |
| Form / waiver labels | 2.4" × 1.5" | 2–3 |
| Sample labels (blood tubes, urine cup) | 2.4" × 1" | 1 per lab service |
| Address label (post-camp mailing) | 2.4" × 3" | 1 (printed in batch, not at check-in) |

**Note on blood draw tube labels:** Standard vacutainer tubes require a narrow wrap-around label (~1"). The 2.4" label folded lengthwise works; confirm fit with your phlebotomist before camp day.

**Alternative if budget allows: Zebra ZD421** (~$450)
- Industry standard in clinical settings
- More durable for frequent use; better if you plan multiple camps per year

## QR Scanners (Check-In & Station Scan)

Volunteer phones work for scanning but are slow under pressure. Dedicated scanners at high-volume points (check-in gate, blood draw) are worth the cost.

**Recommended: Tera 2D Wireless Barcode Scanner** (~$45 each)
- Bluetooth — pairs to any tablet or laptop
- Reads QR codes instantly, even from phone screens
- Long battery life (full day on one charge)
- Buy 2–3: one at check-in, one at blood draw, one spare

**Volunteer phones** can handle lower-volume stations (imaging, physician rooms) using the web app's built-in camera scan — no hardware needed there.

## Tablets (Station Queues & Registration Desk)

**Recommended: iPad (10th generation, 10.9")** (~$350 each)
- Most reliable browser performance for the web app
- Works with the Brother label printer over WiFi
- Good screen visibility in tent / outdoor conditions (high brightness)
- Buy with cases and stands

**How many:**
| Station | Qty |
|---|---|
| Registration / check-in desk | 2 (one for pre-reg check-in, one for walk-in registration) |
| Blood draw | 1 |
| Each physician room | 1 per room (7 for clinic config) |
| Ultrasound / X-ray / EKG | 1 each |
| Injection / medication | 1 |
| Coordinator (god-view) | 1 (can use personal laptop instead) |

**Budget alternative: Samsung Galaxy Tab A9+** (~$220)
- Fine for station queue display; slightly slower browser than iPad
- Use for lower-traffic stations to reduce cost

**Tablet stands: Viozon Tablet Floor Stand** (~$35 each)
- Freestanding, adjustable height — works at a desk or mounted in a cabin doorway
- Buy for each fixed station; volunteers carry handheld at mobile stations

## Waiting Area Display (Open Space Config)

Patients waiting see their camp ID number called on-screen without a volunteer announcing it.

**Recommended: Any 43"–55" smart TV with HDMI** (rent or borrow)
- Plug in a **Google Chromecast with Google TV** (~$50) or any HDMI laptop
- Browser opens the queue display page in fullscreen
- Mount on a portable TV stand or hang on a divider panel

For the clinic config, a single tablet on a stand in the waiting area is sufficient.

## WiFi / Networking

Reliable WiFi is the backbone. Volunteer hotspots are a backup, not a primary plan.

**Clinic config:** Existing clinic WiFi is likely sufficient. Confirm coverage extends to outdoor tents — add one access point to the tent if not.

**Open space config (gym/basketball court):**

**Recommended: TP-Link Deco XE75 3-pack mesh system** (~$250)
- Covers up to 6,900 sq ft — enough for a standard gym with cabins
- WiFi 6 — handles 50+ simultaneous devices without slowdown
- Easy setup: one unit near the internet source, two spread across the floor

**Internet source for open space:** Coordinate with the venue for a hardwired connection. If unavailable:
- **T-Mobile 5G Home Internet router** (~$50/month, no contract) or
- **Verizon Jetpack MiFi 8800L** hotspot — supports 15 devices; use 2–3 of these feeding the mesh network

**Bring regardless:** 3–4 volunteer phones designated as backup hotspots, data plans confirmed before camp day.

## Square Hardware (Payments)

Walk-in payments use **Tap to Pay on a volunteer's phone** (NFC) — no terminal hardware required. Works with Square, Stripe, and PayPal depending on the final payment gateway decision.

| What's needed | Notes |
|---|---|
| Volunteer phone with NFC | iPhone 7+ or most Android phones from 2018+ |
| Payment app installed | Square POS, Stripe Terminal, or PayPal Zettle |
| Internet connection | WiFi or cellular data on the payment phone |

No dedicated card reader or terminal needed.

## Power & Accessories

Often overlooked until something dies mid-camp.

| Item | Qty | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Power strips (6-outlet, surge protected) | 6–8 | One per station cluster |
| Extension cords (25ft, heavy gauge) | 4–6 | For tent / cabin stations far from outlets |
| USB-C charging hubs (6-port) | 2 | Overnight charge all tablets from one plug |
| Portable battery pack (20,000mAh) | 2–3 | Backup for tablets in outdoor tents |
| Label roll spares | 2 rolls | One roll ≈ 400 labels; bring backup |
